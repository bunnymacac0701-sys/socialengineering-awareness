Social engineering is the psychological manipulation of individuals to gain confidential information, system access, or financial gain. Social engineering is dangerous because it turns normal human instincts, like trust and helpfulness, into security weaknesses.

Attackers exploit trust, urgency, fear, and authority instead of breaking encryption or firewalls. Humans are predictable. Attackers just need to understand how people think. It works because we trust authority, respond to urgency and want to be helpful. Social engineering works because it turns your instincts against you.
